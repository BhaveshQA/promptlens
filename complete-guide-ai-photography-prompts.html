The Complete Guide to AI Photography Prompts in 2026

Use this structure to write prompts that feel photographic instead of generic: subject, camera, lens, light, mood, composition, and post-processing.

PromptLens EditorialJan 15, 202612 min read

A strong AI photography prompt behaves like a creative brief. It tells the model what the subject is, what kind of camera language to imitate, how the light should fall, and what mood the final image should carry.

The core formula

Subject + setting + camera + lens + lighting + composition + texture + color grade. You do not need every piece every time, but the more specific the intent, the more repeatable the result.

Copy this starter formula

[Subject] in [setting], shot on [camera], [lens], [aperture], [lighting setup], [composition], [mood], [film stock or color grade], realistic photography, natural detail

Camera specs that help

AI models respond well to camera and lens language because those words imply depth of field, compression, field of view, and texture.

  • 85mm f/1.4 for creamy portrait background blur.
  • 35mm f/1.8 for intimate street and documentary scenes.
  • 14mm f/2.8 for night skies and wide landscapes.
  • 90mm macro for food, product, and close-up detail.

Lighting language

Replace vague words like "beautiful lighting" with usable lighting terms: golden hour backlight, single key light from camera left, soft overcast diffusion, neon reflections, rim light, or Rembrandt lighting.

Example prompts

Portrait

Natural light portrait of a ceramic artist in her studio, window light from camera left, 85mm f/1.4, shallow depth of field, warm skin tones, quiet editorial mood, Kodak Portra 400 color grade

Landscape

Alpine valley at golden hour, mist lifting from pine forest, 24mm wide angle lens, f/8, layered mountain silhouettes, soft amber highlights, realistic landscape photography, high dynamic range

Street

Rainy neon street at night, reflections on wet asphalt, lone cyclist passing a shop window, 35mm documentary photography, motion blur, high ISO grain, cinematic color contrast
